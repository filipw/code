About

Building Quantum Software: A Developer's Guide focuses on utilizing the peculiarities of quantum computing to solve hard problems, rather than discussing quantum mechanics, physics, or natural philosophy. It breaks down the complex concepts of quantum computing into easy-to-understand building blocks, making it accessible to anyone with basic knowledge of programming, trigonometry, and probabilities. No need for a physics degree or advanced math skills - just a curious mind and a desire to learn.

What makes this book special

This book offers practical insights into quantum computing, accessible to readers without advanced mathematical or physics backgrounds. Through hands-on simulations and coding exercises, readers can gain a deeper understanding of quantum computing and learn how to implement quantum solutions on real hardware. Distinguishing between quantum and classical computing is also a key focus, providing readers with a comprehensive understanding of this cutting-edge technology.

How to use this book

This book can be utilized in various ways, including reading the content and inline code, reviewing or executing the Jupyter notebooks in each chapter, completing corresponding exercises, and implementing new experiments in notebooks, unit tests, or more complex applications.
